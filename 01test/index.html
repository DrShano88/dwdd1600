Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology to engage in online communities, encompassing behaviors like respecting others, protecting personal information, understanding digital laws, and promoting positive engagement, much like being a good citizen in the physical world

What is Digital Law?

Digital law (or cyber law) is the body of law regulating activities and relationships in the digital world, covering legal rights and restrictions for using technology and the internet, addressing issues like data privacy, intellectual property, cybersecurity, online speech, and e-commerce

What on Earth is Aup?

An AUP, or Acceptable Use Policy, is a set of rules defining how an organization's digital resources (networks, computers, internet, data) can be used, outlining permitted activities, prohibited behaviors (like illegal downloads or harassment), and consequences for violations to ensure security, compliance, and responsible conduct.

What is Plagiarism?

What Is Plagiarism? | Types of Plagiarism Explained ...Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, work, or creative content as your own without proper acknowledgment, essentially committing literary theft.

Proper citation (quotes, paraphrasing, summarizing) is key to avoiding it.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any deceptive behavior in an educational setting to gain an unfair advantage, including plagiarism (using others' work as your own), cheating (using unauthorized aids), fabrication (making up data), and unauthorized collaboration, often defined in school policies and leading to serious penalties like failing grades or suspension

What are the Rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content in education focus on transparency, academic integrity, data privacy, and responsible use, generally requiring explicit instructor permission, proper citation for any AI assistance (treating unauthorized generation as plagiarism), strict data security (no confidential info in public tools), and critical evaluation of AI outputs for bias or inaccuracies, as policies vary by institution and instructor.
